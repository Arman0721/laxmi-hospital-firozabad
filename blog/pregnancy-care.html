Healthy Pregnancy Care: Trimester to Labor Guidelines

Published: May 28, 2026 By: Dr. Lata Rajput 6 Mins Read

A healthy pregnancy requires active maternal nutrition, systemic vaccination mapping, and periodic clinical consults. From the initial positive urine screen to delivery day, tracking vital fetal markers ensures absolute safety. Under the direction of senior gynecologist Dr. Lata Rajput, we present Firozabad's clinical guide to maternal care.

1. First Trimester (Weeks 1 to 12): Laying The Foundation

The first twelve weeks are critical for embryonic organ development. Fetal growth begins aggressively, requiring rapid cellular building block adjustments:

  • Folic Acid Supplementation: Patients must take 400 mcg of Folic Acid daily to prevent neural tube spinal defects.
  • Early Viability Ultrasound (USG): Scheduled between Weeks 7-9 to confirm fetal heartbeat and rule out ectopic risks.
  • Avoid Self-Medication: Do not take any OTC medication without a gynecologist's consent due to early birth defect risks.

2. Second Trimester (Weeks 13 to 26): The Growth Phase

Fetal structures mature rapidly during this trimester. Clinical screenings focus on detecting structural anomalies early:

  • Anomaly Scan (TIFFA USG): Crucial structural mapping scheduled between Weeks 18-22 to review vital organs.
  • Iron & Calcium Infusions: Prescribed to support expanding maternal blood volumes and fetal skeletal development.
  • Tetanus Toxoid (TT) Vaccines: Mandatory WHO doses administered to prevent infantile neonatal tetanus.

3. Third Trimester (Weeks 27 to 40): Preparing For Delivery

The final weeks focus on monitoring fetal weights, fluid levels (Amniotic fluid index), and birth positioning:

Clinical Focus Area Target Parameters Surgical/Delivery Status
fetal Growth Profile USG weight checking every 3 weeks Ensures optimal birth weight targets.
Blood Pressure Screening BP below 130/80 mmHg Avoids sudden pre-eclampsia delivery risk.
Labor Pain Indicators Regular contractions, fluid leaks Immediate labor room check-in.

4. Vital Maternal Nutrition Targets

Mothers should consume a balanced, high-protein diet incorporating fresh leafy greens, fruits, beans, and calcium-dense milk. Avoid raw or unpasteurized foods to prevent bacterial infections (Listeria). Secure continuous fluid intakes to maintain healthy amniotic volumes.
