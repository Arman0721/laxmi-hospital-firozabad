Preventive Heart Care: Plaque Reduction & Stress Controls

Published: May 02, 2026 By: Dr. Piyush Taneja 5 Mins Read

Coronary artery disease is the leading global cause of sudden death events. Plaque deposits consisting of cholesterol and cellular waste line the arterial walls over time, narrowing blood paths and forcing the heart to pump at dangerously high pressures. Under the direction of Consultant Physician Dr. Piyush Taneja, we present Firozabad's clinical guide to cardiac preservation.

1. Aerobic Exercise: The Cardiac Pumping Booster

Your heart is a muscular pump that responds strongly to exercise. Engaging in **150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity** weekly offers substantial benefits:

  • Vascular Elasticity: Lowers systolic pressure by 5 to 8 mmHg.
  • HDL (Good Cholesterol) Increase: Helps clear LDL plaque back to the liver for digestion.
  • Recommended Workouts: Brisk walking, cycling, or swimming for 30 minutes daily, 5 days a week.

2. Regulating Cholesterol Parameters

A standard lipid profile maps your cardiovascular risk. Keep these targets in check through healthy nutrition and regular screening:

Lipid Parameter Target Clinical Range Cardiology Action Plan
Total Cholesterol Below 200 mg/dL Maintains clear, fluid blood flows.
LDL (Bad) Cholesterol Below 100 mg/dL Prevents arterial blockages.
HDL (Good) Cholesterol Above 40 mg/dL Clears arterial walls.

3. Stress Control & Restful Sleep

Chronic stress releases cortisol and adrenaline, raising heart rates and constricting blood vessels. Manage stress through deep breathing exercises, physical walk breaks, and securing **7 to 8 hours of continuous night sleep** to help regulate cardiac cycles.

4. Warning Triggers: Act Instantly

In cardiac events, time is heart muscle. Do not ignore severe chest pressure, discomfort spreading to the left arm/jaw, or unexplained shortness of breath. Rush to Laxmi Hospital's Resuscitation ward immediately for emergency ECG screening.
