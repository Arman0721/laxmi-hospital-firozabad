Child Health Guide: Essential Immunizations & Nutrition

Published: May 15, 2026 By: Dr. Paras Rajput 5 Mins Read

Securing a child's health requires systematic vaccination checks, clean infant nutrition routines, and proactive growth tracking. Premature and young babies are highly prone to respiratory viruses and gastrointestinal pathogens. Under the direction of child care expert Dr. Paras Rajput, we provide Firozabad's clinical guide to early childhood health.

1. Standard WHO Childhood Vaccine Timelines

Vaccination is the most successful clinical path to prevent severe infantile diseases. Ensure your baby receives these vital immunizations according to the schedule:

  • At Birth: BCG (Tuberculosis protect), Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV-0), and Hepatitis B vaccine.
  • 6, 10, & 14 Weeks: Pentavalent (DPT, Hep B, Hib), Rotavirus vaccine, Pneumococcal Conjugate (PCV), and IPV.
  • 9 Months: MR (Measles-Rubella Vaccine Dose-1) and Vitamin A fluids.
  • 1.5 Years: DPT Booster-1, Polio Booster, and MR Dose-2.

2. Essential Infant Nutrition: Breastfeeding to Weaning

Correct feeding ensures proper physical motor development and cognitive intelligence benchmarks:

  • First 6 Months: Absolute exclusive breastfeeding. No honey, formula milk, or water is needed, as maternal milk provides all vital antibodies.
  • At 6 Months (Weaning): Initiate soft complementary foods (mashed banana, rice gruel, daal soup) while continuing breast milk.
  • Avoid Sugar & Salt: Refrain from adding refined sugars or heavy salts to baby purées before 12 months to prevent kidney load.

3. Childhood Danger Markers: When to Consult Immediately

Seek immediate clinical advice from Dr. Paras Rajput if your child displays any of these warning signs:

Symptom Indicator Clinical Risk Category Pediatric Action
High Fever (>101°F) Moderate Risk Cold sponge, consult pediatrician within 12 hours.
Rapid Breathing / Rib Retractions High Critical Risk Immediate ER nebulization and oxygen check.
Persistent Diarrhea / Lethargy Critical Dehydration Bedside IV fluid re-hydration in pediatric ward.

4. Maintaining A Safe Hygienic Environment

Wash toys frequently, sterilize feeding utensils, and maintain good hand hygiene before feeding infants. Avoid exposing children to passive household smoke or dusty environments to prevent asthma triggers.
